How Much Does YouTube Pay in 2026? Real Numbers

By PlayMETRIC Team  •  Published July 21, 2026  •  Updated July 21, 2026

TL;DR
  • Most monetized channels earn $0.50 to $6 per 1,000 long form views in 2026, which is $0.0005 to $0.006 per view.
  • High-value niches (finance, real estate, business) with US audiences reach $10 to $30 per 1,000 views.
  • YouTube Shorts pay roughly $0.05 to $0.15 per 1,000 views, about 20 to 50 times less than long form.
  • YouTube keeps 45% of ad revenue; creators receive 55%.
  • Subscribers pay nothing directly. Views, niche, and audience country decide your income.

How the payment system actually works

  1. Join the YouTube Partner Program: you need 1,000 subscribers plus either 4,000 public watch hours in 12 months or 10 million Shorts views in 90 days.
  2. Ads run on your videos: advertisers bid in an auction for your viewers. Their bids set your CPM.
  3. YouTube takes 45%: you keep 55% of ad revenue on long form. Shorts use a pooled model with the same 45/55 split after music costs.
  4. AdSense pays monthly: once your balance passes $100, payment arrives between roughly the 21st and 26th of the following month.

What about pay per subscriber?

Nothing. Subscribers do not generate direct revenue. A channel with 100,000 subscribers and no views earns $0. Subscribers matter because they reliably supply views, and views carry the money. As a rough 2026 rule of thumb, an active channel earns somewhere between $1 and $30 per 1,000 subscribers per month depending on upload rate and niche, but that is entirely a side effect of the views those subscribers generate.

Why Shorts pay so little

Shorts ads are pooled across all Shorts and divided by view share, and short videos simply show fewer ads per minute of watch time. At $0.05 to $0.15 per 1,000 views, a million Shorts views typically pays $50 to $150. Shorts are a discovery engine, not an income engine: creators use them to funnel viewers to long form videos where a million views can pay 40x more.

Frequently asked questions

How much does YouTube pay for 1 million views?
In 2026, roughly $500 to $6,000 for most channels, and $10,000 to $30,000 for high-RPM niches like finance with a US audience. For Shorts, a million views typically pays $50 to $150.
How many views do I need to earn $1,000 a month?
At a typical $3 RPM, about 333,000 monthly views. At a finance-niche $18 RPM, about 56,000. Niche is the single biggest lever.
Does YouTube pay more for longer videos?
Videos over 8 minutes can include mid-roll ads, which meaningfully raises revenue per view. Past that, watch time helps indirectly by improving distribution.
When does YouTube pay out?
Monthly through AdSense, usually between the 21st and 26th, once your balance reaches the $100 minimum.
