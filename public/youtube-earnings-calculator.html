How this calculator works

YouTube pays creators through RPM: revenue per 1,000 monetized views, after YouTube takes its 45% share. In 2026, most monetized channels land between $0.50 and $6 RPM overall, but the spread by niche is enormous. Finance, investing, and business content regularly hits $10 to $30 per 1,000 US views because insurance, banking, and investment advertisers pay the most for those viewers. Entertainment, gaming, and music sit at the bottom because advertisers bid less.

Audience geography matters almost as much. A view from the US, UK, Canada, or Australia can be worth 5 to 10 times a view from lower income ad markets. That is why this calculator asks where your audience is instead of pretending every view pays the same.

Frequently asked questions

How much does YouTube pay per 1,000 views in 2026?
For most monetized channels, between $0.50 and $6 per 1,000 views after YouTube's cut. High value niches like finance and investing can reach $10 to $30 per 1,000 views with a US audience, while music and entertainment can fall under $2.
How many views do I need to make $1,000 a month?
It depends entirely on your niche. A finance channel at $18 RPM needs about 56,000 monthly views. A gaming channel at $2.75 RPM needs about 364,000. Use the calculator above with your own niche to get your number.
Do YouTube Shorts pay less than regular videos?
Yes, dramatically. Shorts RPM is typically $0.05 to $0.15 per 1,000 views, roughly 20 to 50 times lower than long form in the same niche. Shorts are great for growth, but long form is where the ad money is.
Is this estimate exact?
No calculator can be exact because YouTube pays based on your specific advertisers, seasonality, and watch time. This tool gives you a realistic range built from published 2026 RPM data by niche and geography, which is much closer than a one-size-fits-all slider.
Does YouTube pay monthly?
Yes. Once you are in the YouTube Partner Program and cross the $100 threshold, AdSense pays out monthly, usually between the 21st and 26th.
